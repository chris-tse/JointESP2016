Accommodation

About Hotels in Japan

Almost all hotels in Japan can be reserved on the internet. The conference strongly recommends the participants to make reservations on the internet instead of phoning the hotels because English may be difficult for some hotel staff. All reservations on the internet have a standard discount. Room rates fluctuate according to the days of the week and room availability. Credit cards are usually acceptable for online reservations. All the hotels recommended by the conference below offer online reservations and accept credit cards.

Attention: In Japan, hotel room rates are usually set based on the number of beds/people reserved, e.g. the rate for a twin room will be based on two people instead of one room.

About Keio Line (Train)

Keio Line, like most downtown trains/subways in Tokyo metropolitan area, has various types of trains such as Special Express, Semi-Special Express, Express, Semi-Express, Rapid, and Local. All types of trains stop at the station of the conference venue, Chofu (Station Number: KO-18), though we do not recommend Rapid and Local trains because they are the slowest. It takes about 14 minutes to 20 minutes to travel between Shinjuku and Chofu stations by express trains. Other types may take twice as long, roughly.

The train fare is the same for all types of Keio trains for the same distance.

2. Shinjuku and Fuchu Areas

Shinjuku Area

Shinjuku area is one of the busiest business and commercial areas in Tokyo. It is also the most convenient location to the conference venue outside of Chofu area. There is a direct train, Keio Line, that goes to UEC Tokyo, the conference venue. It is also convenient location to access other areas of downtown Tokyo. There are many choices of hotels in Tokyo, in addition to the hotels we recommend below.

Fuchu City Area

Fuchu city is a city next to Chofu city farther away from Shinjuku or downtown area to the west direction of Keio Line. It takes roughly 5 minutes by express trains and 11 minutes or so on slower trains.

On Keio Line, it takes 5 or 11 minutes more respectively to go to Shinjuku from Fuchu station (Station Number: 24) than from Chofu station. Since it is the same train line, it is a popular location to stay for people who want to go to Chofu but cannot book a hotel in Chofu.
